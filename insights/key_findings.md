# Key Findings & Business Insights

## Dataset
100,000+ e-commerce orders — Brazil (2016–2018)

## Section I — Exploratory Analysis
- Orders span from October 2016 to October 2018 — just over 2 years
- Customers spread across 27 states and 4,119 unique cities
- Most columns are string type; zip codes stored as integers

## Section II — Order Trends
- Orders grew significantly YoY — strong business growth from 2016 through 2018
- Peak ordering season: November 2017 to March 2018 — holiday season effect
- Afternoon (13:00–18:00) is the peak ordering window across all years

## Section III — State Analysis
- São Paulo (SP) dominates — highest customer count, order volume, and revenue
- States MG, RJ, SP consistently top the charts across all metrics
- RR has the lowest customer count of any state

## Section IV — Economic Impact
- Revenue grew 136.97% from Jan–Aug 2017 to Jan–Aug 2018
- SP leads total order price and freight value by a significant margin
- Freight costs mirror order price distribution — high-volume states pay most in freight

## Section V — Delivery Performance
- Longest single delivery: 209 days — 181 days beyond estimated date
- RR has highest average delivery time; SP has lowest
- AC consistently delivers ahead of estimated date — best logistics performance
- States with faster delivery tend to have higher customer satisfaction scores

## Section VI — Payment Analysis
- Credit card is the dominant payment method across all months and years
- Significant portion of customers use EMI installments — indicates high-value purchases
- Installment usage signals opportunity for targeted financing promotions

## Actionable Recommendations
1. Invest in logistics infrastructure in North/Northeast states — RR delivery times are excessive
2. Target Afternoon slots for promotions — peak ordering window across all years
3. Investigate delivery outliers beyond 30 days — implement alerting system
4. Double down on SP — disproportionate revenue concentration worth protecting
5. Incentivise installment plans — strong existing behaviour worth amplifying
